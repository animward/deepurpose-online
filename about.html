About Deep Purpose

Deep Purpose is a framework for building technology that serves something larger than the next quarterly earnings call. It begins with a question that most builders never ask: what are we actually building, and who does it serve seven generations from now?

The seven principles are not aspirational. They are operational constraints:

  1. Technology is not neutral. Every tool encodes the values of its makers. If you have not examined your values, you are encoding your blindness.
  2. Build for seven generations, not seven sprints. The Haudenosaunee principle applied to software architecture. What does this system look like in 2175?
  3. Consciousness is not a feature. It is the ground on which features are built. Systems that ignore consciousness are systems that ignore their own foundation.
  4. The most dangerous technology is the one that makes you comfortable. Comfort as the final stage of control. The SOLACE thesis applied to product design.
  5. Indigenous knowledge is not a data source. It is a living system with its own sovereignty. Extraction of knowledge without relationship is theft.
  6. If you cannot explain who benefits and who is harmed, you do not understand your own work. Benefit/harm analysis as engineering discipline, not afterthought.
  7. The naming convention is the OPSEC. Say what things are. If you cannot name what you are building truthfully, you should not be building it.

Deep Purpose is part of the EGEX research cluster alongside Cascation, 7G, and VALAROMA. Consciousness-first engineering for those who build with intention.
